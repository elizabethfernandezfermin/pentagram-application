cookbook recipes
graphic design

These recipe chapters were made for my Electronic Design 1 class, where we were tasked with creating the visuals and design of two recipes of our choice. I chose two desserts that are part of my culture because I wanted to visualize the process of making them and their final look. In Dominican culture, Flan and Arroz con Leche are very popular desserts. Using Illustrator, I drew all the ingredients in each recipe and the final look of the food as well.

software:

Indesign, Illustrator.

For the illustrations, I drew them by hand first and then brought them into illustrator to edit them. I changed the outlines from black to white so they would stand out more and blend well with the image of the book. For both recipes, I created a drip-like background, with the specific color of each dessert, to compliment the spread.

For the typefaces, I chose a more handwritten font for the titles, with Relation in bold; For the body I decided to go with a sans-serif to make it legible and modern, using Fira Sans. Another typeface I used exclusively for the numbers was Memimas Pro, which is a dotted typeface, that gives the spreads a more playful feeling and adds to the styling of the spreads.
