mail art book
typography

This mini book was made for my Typography II class, in which we chose what to highlight regarding postal service, ranging from stamp design, mail art, postal history, mail carriers etc. For my theme, I chose mail art, wanting to focus on the timeline of it, and how it developed to what it is today. The book includes two articles about mail art, starting with “How Did Mail Art Develop Into a Global Art Movement” by Angie Kordic followed by “Mail Art is Suddenly Having a Renaissance Amid the Worldwide Lockdown” by Taylor Defoe.

software:

Indesign, Illustrator.

Since the book is meant to be informational, I decided against using complicated fonts for the titles. For the titles I chose Fira Sans as my font, while using Verdana for the body text. For most of the text, I chose to leave it in black, except for the subtitles, some image captions and the by line of the names of the authors. With this, I chose to highlight important parts that would otherwise go unnoticed.

From the cover to the spreads, I placed several hints of the idea of postal service. I took the typical postal envelope with red, white(sometimes beige), and blue distorted rectangles and placed them all throughout. The page number is on top of one, The quotation marks are made up of two red ones, and the titles include a blue distorted rectangle under them.
